# \# Final Recommendation



### \# Executive Summary



This regression analysis was conducted to identify the business factors most strongly associated with monthly sales performance across retail stores. Three regression models were evaluated to determine which analytical approach provides the most reliable support for business decision-making.



The \*\*Multiple Linear Regression Model\*\* was selected as the final model because it achieved the highest explanatory power (\*\*R² = 0.827\*\*) while incorporating multiple operational and business drivers simultaneously. Compared with the two Simple Regression models, it provides significantly stronger predictive capability and more comprehensive business insights.



### \# Key Business Findings



The analysis indicates that the following variables have the strongest positive association with monthly sales:



| Priority | Business Driver        | Strategic Importance |

| -------- | ---------------------- | -------------------- |

| \*\*1\*\*    | Footfall               | Very High            |

| \*\*2\*\*    | Inventory Availability | High                 |

| \*\*3\*\*    | Marketing Spend        | High                 |



These variables consistently demonstrated meaningful relationships with monthly sales and should receive the highest management attention.



### \# Variables Requiring Careful Interpretation



Certain variables should not be interpreted independently because their business impact depends on broader operational context.



Examples include:



\* Region Dummy Variables

\* Store Type Dummy Variables



These variables explain differences between locations and store formats but should be evaluated together with operational performance rather than used as standalone decision factors.

### \# Business Risks and Limitations



Although the selected regression model explains a substantial proportion of sales variation, several external factors remain outside the scope of this analysis, including:



\* Weather conditions

\* Local economic environment

\* Competitor activities

\* Seasonal events

\* Customer demographics

\* Store management quality



Accordingly, business decisions should combine statistical evidence with operational expertise and management judgement.



### \# Association Does Not Imply Causation



Regression analysis identifies \*\*statistical associations\*\* between business variables and monthly sales. It does \*\*not\*\* prove that one variable directly causes changes in another.



Leadership should therefore use regression insights alongside controlled business experiments, operational reviews, and continuous performance monitoring before implementing strategic initiatives.



### \# Final Business Recommendation



Based on the regression analysis, the \*\*Multiple Linear Regression Model\*\* should be adopted as the primary analytical model for supporting retail planning and performance management.



Leadership should prioritize initiatives that:



\* Increase customer footfall.

\* Maintain high inventory availability.

\* Optimize marketing investment.

\* Monitor regional performance differences.

\* Periodically retrain the regression model using updated business data.



This approach provides the strongest evidence-based foundation for improving monthly sales while recognising the practical limitations of statistical modelling.
