# \# Residual Analysis



### \## Executive Summary



Residual analysis was performed on the selected \*\*Multiple Linear Regression Model\*\* to evaluate prediction accuracy and assess whether the model provides reliable estimates of monthly sales. The analysis compares actual monthly sales with predicted sales and identifies observations where the model overestimates or underestimates performance.



The Multiple Regression Model was selected because it achieved the highest explanatory power (\*\*R² = 0.827\*\*) and provided the strongest overall predictive performance among all evaluated models.

### 

### \# Residual Calculation



Residuals were calculated using the following formula:



> \*\*Residual = Actual Monthly Sales − Predicted Monthly Sales\*\*



#### Interpretation:



\* \*\*Positive Residual:\*\* Actual sales are higher than predicted by the model.

\* \*\*Negative Residual:\*\* Actual sales are lower than predicted by the model.

\* \*\*Residual close to zero:\*\* Model prediction closely matches actual sales.

### 

### \# Largest Positive Residuals



The records with the highest positive residuals represent stores where actual sales exceeded model predictions.



#### \## Business Interpretation



Possible reasons include:



\* Exceptional local customer demand

\* Highly effective store management

\* Successful regional marketing campaigns

\* Strong customer loyalty

\* Competitive advantages not captured in the available dataset



#### \### Recommended Action



Management should investigate these high-performing stores to identify successful operational practices that can be replicated across other locations.



### \# Largest Negative Residuals



The records with the largest negative residuals represent stores where actual sales were significantly below model predictions.



#### \## Business Interpretation



Possible reasons include:



\* Product stock-outs

\* Strong local competition

\* Operational inefficiencies

\* Reduced customer satisfaction

\* Temporary regional or economic disruptions

#### 

#### \### Recommended Action



Conduct operational reviews to identify root causes and implement corrective actions to improve future store performance.



### \# Residual Pattern Assessment



Overall, the residuals appear reasonably balanced around zero, indicating that the Multiple Regression Model provides reliable predictions for most stores.



However, several observations exhibit comparatively large positive or negative residuals, suggesting that additional business factors influence sales but are not included in the current regression model.



#### Potential missing variables include:



\* Weather conditions

\* Local economic environment

\* Competitor promotions

\* Customer demographics

\* Store management quality

\* Seasonal events



### \# Model Performance Assessment



The residual analysis indicates that:



\* Prediction errors are generally moderate.

\* No obvious systematic bias is observed.

\* The model performs consistently across most observations.

\* Extreme residuals are limited to a relatively small number of stores.



These findings support the suitability of the Multiple Regression Model for business forecasting and decision support.



### \# Limitations



Residual analysis evaluates prediction accuracy but cannot identify every factor affecting store performance.



Regression models should therefore be used alongside operational knowledge, business expertise, and continuous performance monitoring.



### \# Business Conclusion



The residual analysis confirms that the \*\*Multiple Regression Model\*\* is appropriate for supporting retail planning and performance management.



Leadership should use model predictions together with operational insights rather than relying solely on statistical outputs. Future model enhancements should incorporate additional external variables to further improve predictive accuracy and strengthen business decision-making.
